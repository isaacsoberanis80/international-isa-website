# International ISA — Marketing Strategy

Working strategy doc. Built 2026-07-02 from market research (see also
`LEARNING_LOG.md` and the memory notes on this project). Update this as
real client data comes in — right now several numbers here are industry
benchmarks, not International ISA's own results yet.

---

## 1. Positioning

**Who we are:** bilingual back-office and sales support for real estate
agents, contractors, and service businesses (construction, plumbing,
electrical, commercial), professional firms (architects, engineers), and
insurance agencies who want to grow without hiring in-house.

**The honest differentiator:** not price. AI voice-agent tools and
low-cost overseas VA shops (~$8/hr for generic construction VAs, per
market research) are already undercutting human staffing on cost. We
can't and shouldn't try to win a price war against a $1-2k/month AI
answering service. The real edge is:
- **Bilingual, relationship-driven selling** — not scripted, not robotic.
- **Sales-trained, not just clerical** — the difference between someone
  who answers a phone and someone who qualifies and closes.
- **Cross-industry judgment** — understands both a real estate deal and a
  contractor's job site well enough to represent either credibly.

**One-line pitch:** "The back-office team behind growing businesses —
bilingual sales and admin support, without the overhead of hiring."

## 2. Target Segments (ranked by buyer-readiness)

1. **Real estate teams/agents already spending on lead gen** — the
   clearest fit, closest to the original PowerISA-style model. Buyer-ready
   signal: coaches commonly recommend ~500+ active prospects in pipeline
   before an ISA makes sense — that's a good qualifying question.
2. **Teams *currently hiring* an in-house ISA** — the hottest lead type.
   They've already budgeted for this exact problem; the pitch is
   "outsource it instead of hiring, no payroll/turnover risk."
3. **Contractors/trades (plumbing, electrical, general construction)**
   drowning in calls, quotes, and scheduling — validated by a real,
   active market of competitors (Virtual Nexgen, My BTLR, StealthAgents,
   AnswerForce) already selling into this exact pain point.
4. **Insurance agencies** — a strong fit and a segment the founder has
   direct, credible experience in (ran commercial insurance accounts
   full-cycle at ProTech: policy data, claims support, carrier
   cross-shopping). Real driver: the insurance outsourcing market grew to
   ~$9.9B in 2025 at ~7% CAGR, pushed partly by rising compliance
   complexity — agencies are outsourcing admin/support work to keep up,
   not just to save money. Lead with the founder's own insurance
   background in outreach to this segment specifically.
5. **Architecture & engineering firms** — a real niche opportunity,
   *not* competing with existing architectural outsourcing (those
   companies do CAD/BIM/design work, not sales or admin). International
   ISA's angle here is different and underserved: managing client intake,
   scheduling, quotes, and follow-up for firms that have no interest in
   outsourcing the design work itself.
6. **Bilingual-market businesses specifically** — any of the above that
   are currently *losing* Spanish-speaking customers because nobody on
   staff can properly serve them. This is a distinct, underserved angle
   none of the AI-answering-service competitors can credibly claim.

## 3. Competitive Landscape

| Type | Examples | How we're different |
|---|---|---|
| Real estate ISA staffing | PowerISA | We're multi-industry, not real-estate-only; same "trained human, not just a warm body" positioning |
| AI voice/answering services | LeadTruffle, Whippy, AnswerForce, ReceptionHQ | We're human + bilingual + sales-trained, not just call triage |
| Generic offshore VAs | Virtual Nexgen, My BTLR, StealthAgents | We're sales-capable and bilingual, not just clerical support at commodity rates |
| Architectural/engineering outsourcing | RemoteAE, WorldTeams, Lynx Pros | They outsource the *design* work (CAD, BIM); we handle sales/admin around it — different service, not a competitor |
| Insurance BPO/back-office outsourcing | Sonant, Artex Risk | Mostly claims-processing/compliance-focused; we lead with sales and client relationship work, backed by the founder's actual insurance production experience |

**Implication:** don't compete on being the cheapest. Compete on being the
credible middle ground — more capable than a bot, more affordable and
flexible than a full-time bilingual hire.

## 4. Marketing Channels (bootstrapped, in priority order)

1. **Direct outreach to hot leads** (see Prospecting Playbook below) — the
   highest-leverage, zero-cost channel available right now.
2. **LinkedIn** — post about the "outsource instead of hire" angle,
   comment on real estate/contractor posts about scaling pains. Free,
   slow-building, credibility-focused.
3. **Local trade associations & real estate boards** — many hold events or
   have member directories; a booth/sponsorship or even just membership
   puts International ISA in front of qualified buyers directly.
4. **Referral ask, once there are 1-2 real clients** — service businesses
   run in trust-based networks; one happy client referring another is
   worth more than most paid channels at this stage.
5. **Paid ads (later, not now)** — Google LSA / Facebook targeting real
   estate teams and contractors is viable once there's a case study to
   point to. Not the first move with zero social proof.

## 5. Prospecting Playbook (how to actually find people to contact)

**Tactic A — "They're already hiring" signal (highest priority):**
Search Indeed/ZipRecruiter/Glassdoor for `"inside sales agent" real
estate` or `"ISA" real estate remote` filtered to recent postings. Teams
actively posting these jobs have already identified and budgeted for the
exact problem International ISA solves. Sample findings from this
session: multiple active Florida and Southern California teams hiring
ISAs in the $50-200K range, several with immediate start dates — this
list refreshes constantly, so re-run the search regularly rather than
treating any snapshot as final.

**Tactic B — RealTrends "America's Best Real Estate Teams":**
realtrends.com publishes ranked lists (small/medium/large/mega/enterprise
tiers) of real production teams. The medium tier is the sweet spot —
big enough to have ISA budget, small enough that a founder/team lead is
still reachable directly rather than buried in a large org. (Note: this
page loads its results via JavaScript, so pulling it requires viewing it
directly in a browser rather than a quick fetch — worth doing by hand.)

**Tactic C — Trade/contractor directories:**
Angi, Thumbtack, and Google Local Services Ads profiles show which
plumbers/electricians/contractors are actively running paid lead-gen in a
given area — a strong signal they have call/lead volume worth managing
professionally. Local licensing board directories (varies by state/
province) are another free source of contractor contact info.

**Tactic D — Bilingual-market angle:**
Look specifically for businesses in markets with a large Spanish-speaking
population (e.g., South Florida, Texas, Southern California, parts of
BC) that don't currently list bilingual service — a concrete, provable
gap International ISA closes immediately.

**Tactic E — Insurance agencies via warm network:**
This is the founder's own former industry (ProTech). Start with direct,
personal outreach to former colleagues/carrier contacts before cold
prospecting — a warm "I used to do this, now I help agencies like yours
outsource it" pitch will land better than a cold one, and it's a channel
none of the other segments have.

**Tactic F — Local architecture/engineering firm directories:**
State licensing boards (architects and engineers are licensed
professions almost everywhere) publish public directories of licensed
firms — a clean, free source of contact targets. Prioritize small/
mid-size firms (5-30 people); large firms likely already have in-house
admin staff.
