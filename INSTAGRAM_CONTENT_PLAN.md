# Instagram Content Plan — International ISA

Goal: attract real estate agents (and secondarily trades/service owners)
with genuinely useful content, and let the "delegate your follow-up" message
land softly — never a hard pitch. We sell the outcome (won-back hours,
answered leads), not the service, in 9 out of 10 posts.

**Cadence:** 4 posts/week (Mon/Wed/Fri + 1 Reel), 15 min/day engagement
(comment on 5 agent/broker posts in target markets). Consistency beats
volume — do not start a 7-day/week plan that dies in week 2.

**Language:** English-first. One Spanish post per week maximum, marked as a
plus ("hablamos español"), never the identity of the account.

---

## The 3 content pillars

### Pillar 1 — "Speed wins deals" (educational, 50%)
Tips agents can use TODAY about lead follow-up and conversion. This is the
value magnet, and every tip quietly proves why delegating follow-up matters.

Post ideas (each = 1 carousel or Reel):
1. "The 5-minute rule: a lead contacted within 5 minutes is ~9x more likely
   to convert. Here's a follow-up cadence that actually gets done."
2. "Day 1 / Day 4 / Day 10: the follow-up schedule we run for every lead"
   (yes — share our real cadence, it builds authority).
3. "3 voicemail scripts that get callbacks" (give real scripts).
4. "What to say when a lead says 'just send me info'" (objection handling).
5. "Your CRM is lying to you: 4 signs your pipeline is full of dead leads."
6. "How top teams answer every call without the agent picking up" (subtle).
7. "The math of a missed call: what one unanswered lead costs at your
   average commission."
8. "Open house follow-up: the 48-hour window most agents waste."

### Pillar 2 — "Behind the desk" (trust/founder, 30%)
Founder-led content. People buy from people, especially from new companies
with no testimonials yet.

Post ideas:
9. "Why I started International ISA" (founder story, 60-sec Reel).
10. "What a day of professional follow-up actually looks like" (screen-free
    walkthrough: the cadence board, the call list — our real dashboard).
11. "We're new. Here's why we publish our pricing anyway." ($18/hr,
    transparency as the differentiator).
12. "We built our own CRM and AI assistant to run our business — the same
    discipline we bring to yours." (proof-of-work, photos of the dashboard).
13. Honest build-in-public updates: "Week 3 of outreach: what's working."

### Pillar 3 — "Delegation math" (soft sell, 20%)
The only openly commercial pillar. Always framed as math/outcomes.

Post ideas:
14. "$45,000/year hire vs $1,560/month desk: the real cost comparison."
15. "15 hours a week: what agents told us admin actually costs them."
16. "Quiz: should you hire, delegate, or automate? 5 questions."
17. "What we'd take off your plate in week one" (concrete task list).
18. Direct CTA post (1x/month max): "We have capacity for 2 more teams
    this month. Free 15-min call, link in bio."

## Formats that work in 2026
- **Reels 30–60s** with captions burned in (most agents scroll muted).
- **Carousels** for scripts/checklists (highest saves — saves = reach).
- **A face beats a logo**: founder on camera outperforms graphics. Even
  voice-over + screen recording of the dashboard beats stock images.
- Every post ends with a question or a save-prompt ("Save this for your
  next open house"), not a pitch.

## Hashtags & keywords
Core: #realestateagent #realtorlife #realestateteam #insidesales #isa
#leadgeneration #realestateleads #followup #realtortips #realestatecoach
Rotate 8–12 per post; put the keyword phrase ("inside sales agent for real
estate") in the caption text itself — Instagram search reads captions now.

Bio line (searchable): "Inside Sales Agents for real estate teams &
service businesses | Your leads, followed up | $18/hr | EN/ES"

## Distribution beyond the feed
- Repurpose every carousel as a LinkedIn post (agents' brokers live there).
- DM (never cold-pitch) every agent who saves/comments: "Glad it was
  useful — what's your current follow-up setup?" Conversation, not pitch.
- Offer the Day 1/4/10 cadence as a free PDF via DM keyword ("CADENCE") —
  builds a warm list without ads.

## What NOT to do
- No fake testimonials, stock "results", or invented numbers — same policy
  as the website.
- No buying followers; agents check.
- No daily posting you can't sustain. 4/week, every week, wins.
- Don't lead with bilingual/Spanish — it's a closing detail, not the hook.
