# International ISA — Outreach Templates

Ready-to-send templates, one per segment from `MARKETING_STRATEGY.md`.
Fill in the `[bracketed]` parts before sending — a template that's
obviously copy-pasted defeats the point. Keep the tone the site uses:
direct, human, not scripted.

---

## 1. Real estate teams — found via an active "Inside Sales Agent" job posting

Use when: you found a team currently hiring an in-house ISA (Tactic A).
This is your hottest lead type — they've already budgeted for the exact
problem you solve.

**Subject:** Saw you're hiring an ISA — a faster option?

**Email:**

> Hi [Name],
>
> I saw [Team/Brokerage Name] is hiring an Inside Sales Agent right now —
> congrats on the growth. Before you go through the full hiring, training,
> and ramp-up process, wanted to put an alternative in front of you:
> International ISA supplies trained, bilingual ISAs who plug straight
> into your team — no recruiting, no payroll, no turnover risk if it's
> not the right fit.
>
> Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week to see if it's a
> match? Happy to work around your schedule.
>
> [Your name]
> International ISA — [phone] — [site URL]

**LinkedIn DM (shorter):**

> Hi [Name] — saw the ISA posting for [Team Name]. I run International
> ISA, bilingual inside sales agents for real estate teams, no hiring
> required. Worth a quick 15-min call before you commit to the hiring
> process?

## General notes

- **Always personalize the first line** — reference something specific
  (the job posting, the platform they're active on, the shared history).
  Generic openers get ignored or flagged as spam.
- **Keep it to one ask**: a 15-minute call. Don't try to close the deal
  in the first message.
- **Track what you send** — log it in the dashboard's task list
  (`/dashboard`) so nothing falls through: who, when, which template,
  and the response.
- **No fabricated results in outreach either** — same rule as the
  website. Don't claim client outcomes that don't exist yet; lean on your
  own background (Comcast, ProTech, this website itself) as the proof
  point until real client results exist.
